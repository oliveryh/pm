## 5AM Strategy

Currently I've been waking up at 5am to complete the Advent of Code questions. Along with waking up at 5, I've done the following:

* Started using the app **Focus To-Do**
* Leaving infrequent morning schedule tasks like shaving to the end of the day
* Going to the gym every morning after the AoC question.

### Benefits

* Waking up more easily
* Generally sleeping better
* Being a pragmatic and positive person
* Making time for the gym, reading, meditation

### Rooms for Improvement

* Not forgetting your suit for work (use checklists again)
* Forgetting to kick off Focus To-Do app
* Further simplify tasks so they can be incorporated into Focus To-Do
* Going to sleep early enough to ensure 8 hours of sleep

### Thoughts

It seems that this strategy puts a few quick wins at the beginning of my day. Perhaps, once the Advent of Code competition has ended, I could replace this with reading or some other skill acquisition in coding. The important thing is that I make it time critical, it must be started at 5am.

While Advent of Code has been fun, and does force me to get up at 5am, the challenges have become quite hard, and there is less guarantee that I'll be able to finish them. Since my expertise aren't quite there, I may benefit more from learning a python data structure for and hour than struggling with an AoC question.

Ideas for morning tasks (1h):

* Read a chapter of book (reliable time, always beneficial)
* Work on ongoing programming project
* Writing for blog
