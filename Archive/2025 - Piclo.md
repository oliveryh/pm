# 2025 - Piclo

Oliver worked at Piclo as a software engineer and lead engineer, line managing a small team building flexibility market tooling for the energy grid.

---

## Colleagues

Notes from line management and 1:1s.

### Stu Cox

Engineering lead / manager. Oliver's line manager in early years.

- Recommended *Getting to Yes* for negotiations — when disagreeing, move up the abstraction ladder until you find common ground, then work down
- Line management framing: focus on a short set of aims (career development, person-role fit) rather than an exhaustive activity list
- Suggested Oliver focus on coaching and planning as a path toward tech lead
- Piclo contracts are intentionally vague (no explicit requirements list) to avoid penalty clauses

### Josh Mollart

Senior engineer / team lead.

- Integrated well socially, took initiative on process improvements
- Comfortable with external stakeholders early on
- Key feedback style: state intent/goal upfront before asking bigger questions

### Olga Chlebus

Frontend engineer, previously on the platform team. Interested in tooling-oriented FE work.

### Jack Shaw

Wanted to spend more time on feature testing and switch removal; frustrated by volume of demo/training prep pulling him away from engineering.

### Scott McGinnigle

Engineering lead on Flex Gateway team. Noted that some engineers preferred working with fewer acceptance criteria on-the-fly, contrasting with Flex Gateway's approach of thorough upfront scoping.

### Lorna Wright

People/HR partner. Helped navigate team engagement and management situations.

- KM salary context (2023): £55k aligned to late Level 2 / early Level 3

### Ben Reynolds

Backend engineer.

- Strengths: deep diving, backend focus, interest in GIS
- Weaknesses: inconsistent energy sprint-to-sprint, prefers autonomy — doesn't respond well to being probed or micromanaged
- Slow to warm up to discovery sessions; thrives when given a clear task to own
- Communication around ticket progress was a recurring issue

### Elliot Redhead

Full-stack engineer. Line managed by Oliver 2023–2024.

- Cared deeply about understanding user needs before building; joined for "tech for good" mission
- Felt personal development was often a tickbox exercise rather than meaningful
- Preferred async communication (screenshots, video) over live demos or adlibbed Show & Tell
- Wanted intent/goals stated clearly before being asked big questions
- Interested in FE upskilling but didn't want to be exclusively FE
- Left Piclo by late 2024

### Kayleigh Maynard

Full-stack engineer. Line managed by Oliver from 2022.

- Capable and dependable, good attention to detail in code reviews
- Voice in meetings was quieter but contributions were usually insightful
- Recurring pattern: camera off during sessions, limited engagement in team days and office attendance
- Joined 2022 as Apprentice CPF level; by 2024 scored 3.6 — effectively an established Level 3
- Moved into Flex Gateway team in 2024; performance visibly improved there
- Felt suggestions were sometimes shut down, impacting motivation
- Wanted more autonomy to shape parts of the product rather than being told what to work on
- Felt detached during discoveries by mid-2025; attributed to lack of ownership

**End of probation feedback summary:** Strong on collaboration and code quality; encouraged to lead more scoping sessions and speak up more in group discussions.
