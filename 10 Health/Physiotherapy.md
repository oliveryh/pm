# Background

Mobility is incredibly important especially in an age where a large proportion of the population work in office jobs and therefore a sedentary lifestyle. Intentional practice in mobility is therefore essential to ensure one doesn't have problems later in life.

# Tips

## Gym Training

### Contralateral Stabilisation

- Use a split stance (foot forward opposite to the extending arm)
- Removes likelihood that neck muscles will be engaged

### Tilted Pelvis

- Tile pelvis back when doing a check press
- Keep back flat
- Raise knees as well, to further reduce strain on traps

### Negatives

- Focus on negatives
- This will increase strength and reduce the likelihood of having bad form
- Safer

### Low Squats

- There is no need to go well bellow the horizontal on a squat
- This no longer engages glutes and instead puts strain on the spinal column

# Exercises

## Deep Neck Flexor

[Deep Neck Flexor Exercises | Sprint Physiotherapy Ltd.](https://www.sprintphysio.co.uk/patient-exercises/back-and-neck/deep-neck-flexor-exercises.html)

When your body isn't properly stacked, your deep neck flexor is overworked.

### Supine Chin Tuck

1. Lie down flat with legs comfortable bent
2. Without lifting your head up, perform a **small nodding movement** to look towards your toes.
3. Repeat 10 times. Hold for 5 seconds

You shouldn't be able to feel any activity from your [[Sternocleidomastoid]] from this activity.

#### Progressions

1. You can hold this for progressively more time
2. Start to perform this vertically

### Supine Chin Curl

Similar process to the tuck, however at the extension, the head can them be curled.

## Bent Arm Wall Stretch

- Stand with arm bent at 90 degrees
- Face wall
- Rest arm on wall and lean into stretch (should feel this in pec minor)

## First Rib Stretch

- Put fingers in crevice of neck to be able to feel first rib on a light stretch
- Turn the head **lightly** away from the hand and hold for 10 seconds
- Move the head 30 degrees either direction and repeat

## Cable Stretch

- Use the cable machine and do a split stretch chest press forward

**Learn to splay toes**
Splaying toes can increase the structure of the arch
Born to Walk book also suggests that splaying toes helps engage supporting muscles when walking

Active toe spread out exercises. Just spread out your toes back and forth.

Generally recommend barefoot shoes to accommodate the natural shape of the feed. Toe spreaders can also be used to help encourage toe movement

**Single Leg Balance**
We can train single leg stability by balancing on one leg. Static balancing for a minute on each side for 3 sets. Heal raises (6 seconds up and 6 seconds down) can also be combined

**Absorb Impact Forces**
We need to train our foot shock absorbers through the action of pronation - The controlled flattened of our foot arch.

Exercise: Take a small towel, fold it into a rectangle and roll into a sausage shape. Place it between ankles and jump up and down. The towel will reduce the likelihood of over-pronation from occurring. Hop to the beat of a 160BPM to replicate a running cadence. Add weights or a skipping rope or add toe spaces as a progression.

**Barefoot Exercise**
A _gradual_ introduction to barefoot exercises appear to be a good way to increase their foot strength.

Single Leg Supinated Heel Raise: Start by angling your toes inwards at a 45 degree angle. Balance on one leg and press your toes into the ground with force until your heal lifts.
