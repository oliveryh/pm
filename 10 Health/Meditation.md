## Motivations for Meditation

My motivation to meditate is to not only have peace within my own mind, but be present for those I interact with. Staying calm in a stressful situation is one way in which this is effective.

There is a school of thought that says that in bad situations, you cannot control what happens to you, only how you deal with it. I believe this is largely linked to *stoicism*, which is largely why I wish to study that further.

# Guides

## Mental Noting

Mentally noting things such as the breath help up stay focused on it. For example you could say 'in/out' or count up in ones for every breath.

## Being Present

The guide to being present during the day, can be summarised as the following

- Think carefully what you're about to do before you sit or stand
- Understand what life category the activity may fit into and remain in that state of mind, casting out distractions until the task is complete

### Sitting and Standing

There's a focus in this meditations on the transition of sitting to standing and visa versa. I believe it is often because this is the action we take before we change what we're doing. I should show an intentionality in this movement and the knowledge that I know exactly what I'm about to do before I get up and do it. This will reduce the likelihood of being distracted.
