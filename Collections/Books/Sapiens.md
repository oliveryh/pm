## Chapter 2

### Cognitive Revolution

The 'Cognitive Revolution' is accordingly the point when history declared its independence from biology.

Before this biology set the basic parameters for the behaviours and capabilities of Homo Sapiens. It is thought that gossiping is the reason in which we desired human language. With the ability to share information about who to trust we could build bigger groups. However when these groups got any bigger than 150, there would be too much chaos.

The ability to invent fiction has allowed us to coexist in entire kingdoms or societies. Two lawyers who have never met can combine efforts to find a complete stranger because they both believe in the existence of law.

This joint network of stories, we call common 'imagined realities'. It is not a lie that it is illegal to kill someone in the U.K. and as such, we began to live better the realities of biology and humankind.

### Bypassing The Genome

While the behaviour pattern of archaic humans remained fixed for tens of thousands of years, sapiens could transform their social structures, the nature of their interpersonal relations, their economic activities and a host of other behaviours within a decade or two.

We are able to change behaviours within the species massively within a single lifetime.
