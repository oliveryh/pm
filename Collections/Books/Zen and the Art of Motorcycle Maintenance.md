## Part II

The author talks about inductive and deductive logic in order to navigate the classic reasoning of a system. Inductive reasoning is based on observations, while reductive reasoning based on known facts about a system.

The scientific method seeks to find a single answer to a hypothesis. Given the increasing capabilities of scientific discovery, the lifetime of a hypothesis is reduced. Through multiplication of facts and theories, science is leading mankind from single absolute truth to multiple, indeterminate, multiple ones.

### Empiricism

Suppose a child is born of all senses and lives to 18 years of age, he is fed intravenously. Does this child have a thought in his head?

Hume, an empiricist, believes all knowledge is derived exclusively from the senses. If one is asked to describe metal, we struggle to simply list it's composition or appearances in terms of observation.

Kant says there are aspects of reality which sure not supplied immediately by the senses. He calls these _a priori._ An example of _a priori_ knowledge is time or space as you cannot empirically sense these things, but rather apply the these concepts to impressions we perceive.

An example of this separation of knowledge is a 'Copernician Revolution'. There existed two a priori concepts that the earth revolved around the sun or visa versa, and yet the discovery that we lived in a heliocentric universe did not change our observations/senses.
