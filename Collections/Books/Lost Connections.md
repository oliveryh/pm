# Notes

## Chapter 2: Disconnections from other people

John Caccioppo developed the idea of social neuroscience whereby we consider the neuroscience of someone as a product of the experiences and interactions they have.

One key finding from this is that loneliness procludes anxiety and depression in most cases. Additionally loneliness can lead to higher rates of disease, cancer and death. The rates of the stress hormone cortisol are similar to that of experiencing a physical attack when you're extremely lonely.

> Lonely people are scanning for threats because they unconsciously know that nobody is looking out for them, so no one will ever help them if they are hurt. The snowball effect, he learnt, can be reversed but to help a depressed or severely anxious person out of it, they need to have more love and more reassurance than they would ever have needed in the first place. The tragedy, John realised, is that many depressed and anxious people receive less love, as they become harder to be around. indeed, they receive judgement, and criticism, and this accelerates their retreat from the world.

Loneliness it seems is the ability to experience something with someone that is mutually important to that person. This is why you may be in the city, but feel lonely because no one cares about you or your life.

Despite the advent of [[Social Media]], we have replaced the loneliness we feel in our real lives with the artificial promise of connection we get online, effectively losing our tribes.

> "People must belong to a tribe" - E. O. Wilson

## Chapter 3: Loss of Meaningful Values

There are two kinds of motives:
1. Intrinsic Motives - The things you do purely because you value them in and out themselves
2. Extrinsic Motives - Things you don't want to do but you'll get something in return

Studies suggest that the more materialistic and extrinsically motivated you become, the more depressed and anxious you become.

This is likely due to the fact that we have certain innate needs such as feeling valued, connected, and secure and chasing materialism and capitalism inherently exposes these needs as not being enough. We're telling ourselves that we're not connected enough when we compare how many friends someone has on social media.

## Chapter 12: Cause Seven: Disconnection from a Hopeful or Secure Future

The Italian Virno coined the terms _proletariat_ to mean a solid block of manual workers with jobs and _precariat_ to mean a shifting mass of chronically insecure people without job security.

It seems the move to a gig economy and zero hour contracts is causing people to be depressed as they can't see any version of themselves in the future. They can't plan holidays or see a secure future.

The inability to control your future was also a contributing factor to levels of depression and anxiety.
