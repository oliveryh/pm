## Key Gains & Losses Narrative

### 2021 — Steady Start

The ISA launched in April 2021 with £20,000 and performed consistently well through the year, with most months delivering modest positive returns. Global markets were buoyant post-pandemic, supported by low interest rates and fiscal stimulus. The portfolio grew from £20,000 to around £27,000 by year end, a strong first year.

### 2022 — The Rate Shock

2022 was the most turbulent period for the portfolio. Central banks globally — including the US Federal Reserve and Bank of England — aggressively hiked interest rates to combat surging inflation. The Fed alone raised rates seven times, from near zero to 4.25–4.5%. Global equities fell sharply, and cumulative returns briefly turned **negative** in June and September 2022, meaning the portfolio was worth less than the total amount invested. Key loss months:
- **Jan 2022:** -£1,690
- **Jun 2022:** -£1,380
- **Sep 2022:** -£1,752
- **Dec 2022:** -£1,753

### 2023 — Recovery

Despite widespread recession fears, the global economy proved resilient. Inflation began to fall, and markets anticipated future rate cuts. The portfolio steadily recovered through 2023, with cumulative returns climbing from near zero back above £5,000 by year end. Notable gains in November and December helped close the year strongly.

### 2024 — Momentum Builds

2024 was an excellent year for global equities, with the S&P 500 returning over 23%. AI-driven technology stocks were a significant driver. The portfolio grew consistently, with cumulative returns reaching nearly £15,000 by year end. Only April and September saw minor pullbacks.

### 2025 — Volatile but Strong

2025 brought significant turbulence. US "Liberation Day" tariff announcements in April triggered the biggest trade policy shock since the 1930s, causing sharp market falls — reflected in the portfolio's worst single month: **March 2025 at -£4,538**. However, markets rebounded strongly from May onwards, with five consecutive months of gains peaking at **October 2025 (+£3,318)**. The year ended with cumulative returns at a high of ~£23,000 before a late pullback.

### 2026 — Uncertainty Continues

Early 2026 has been choppy. January was flat, February recovered (+£1,621), but March saw the second worst month on record (-£4,448), likely driven by continued geopolitical tensions and tariff uncertainty. April is already showing signs of recovery (+£770 so far).
