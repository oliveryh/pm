## Scenarios

### Low Effort Month

- 4 days a week of 7k walks = 3 * 4 * 4 = 48 points
- 1 gym session a week = 5 * 1 * 4 = 20

So that’s 68 points a month. So I’ll be paying £7 a month for 36 months = £252. Alongside the initial cost of £39, that totals 252+39 = £291 (The RRP is apparently £419).

## High Effort Month

- 4 days a week of 10k walks = 5 * 4 * 4 = 80 points
- 3 gym sessions or 30 minute exercise a week = 5 * 3 * 4 = 60

So that’s 140 points a month. So I’ll be paying £2 a month for 36 months = £72. Alongside the initial cost of £39, that totals 72+39 = £111

## Tips from Colleagues

1. Annually you can get hundreds of points just by completing their online health checks
2. Weekly you can earn:
    1. 40 movement points, I split this into 2 x 3 point days, 2 x 5 point days and 3 x 8 point days:
        1. Optimise your workouts:
            1. If you've been cycling for 28 mins do 2 more!
            2. If you've done 6500 steps before bed do 500 more!
            3. I can get an 8 point workout by washing the cars or doing housework or gardening for an hour
            4. An hour brisk walk is 8 points and takes less time than 12500 steps (which is also worth 8 points)
            5. Lifting in the gym is harder to keep heart rate up than cardio, so mix it up
            6. Know your 60% and 70% heart rates to ensure you stay above them in work outs
            7. You can only earn 160 points per month, so plan your workouts around month end (leave it until the 1st if you've already got 160 this month)
            8. Pause your workouts when you need to to keep your average heart rate higher, for example if you stop in the street to talk to a neighbour?
    2. 6 mindfulness points - use Calm or otherwise to do 3 x 10 min sessions per week
3. Points thresholds for status' are lower or easier when there's more than one of you on the policy.
