## Highlights

- No, a lunch break doesn’t just mean eating lunch with one hand while typing with the other (I remind myself of this often). You’re only making your [brain work harder](https://www.forbes.com/sites/alankohll/2018/05/29/new-study-shows-correlation-between-employee-engagement-and-the-long-lost-lunch-break). Step away from your computer and savor your breaks. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01h7k7dze19tfd3rhw1dqzz71k))
- Even if you only have a small amount of time to spare, use your break time to stretch out, make a cup of coffee, check your phone, rub your dog’s belly, or whatever you need to return to your work feeling motivated enough to power through until your next break. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01h7k7e7wt5qjmn4py83x9z8w6))
- I’m not a big fan of surprises, so I set aside the last half hour of my day to review the next day’s calendar, making note of any meetings I have and reviewing any pertinent agendas to give myself some time to prepare for what’s ahead. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01h7k7grcrpses55g3j5t9br4t))
- Preparing for meetings ahead of time is especially helpful on days when I have back-to-back meetings, because I can refer to the list of questions or talking points I drafted the day before, even if I’m winding down from a meeting I had earlier. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01h7k7h1hk0qch6mjf8e1mx65d))
