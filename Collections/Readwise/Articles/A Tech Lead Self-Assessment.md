## Highlights

- I decided it would be a good idea to do a more formal exploration of technical leadership. I found some great resources: I’m making my way through [staffeng.com](https://staffeng.com), joined [Rands Leadership Slack](https://randsinrepose.com/welcome-to-rands-leadership-slack/), and found some good reddit threads (particularly on [r/ExperiencedDevs](https://www.reddit.com/r/ExperiencedDevs/)). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfsydzfn0q5hbgz18jwnce))
- Estimate bandwidth, understand priorities, and say no to lower priority tasks. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfv88x3fgan631y3vq2m39))
- Skip or delegate meetings that are not necessary. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfvcdrp7swfjjx3agg6ggj))
- Be realistic about workload and avoid working on two jobs simultaneously. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfvhrfg7kk4mv08zq65ryr))
- Delegate to your team and allow them to succeed or fail. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfw03vgexb3jjm8gcmevfk))
- When you delegate, don’t micromanaging every detail. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfw4mh5tfjehre44v6t2j1))
- Focus on the team supporting itself; let juniors work with each other before approaching the tech lead. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfwafrsvyg4szy32v6nqy0))
- Recognize your team members publically for their successes. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfwh67qjnt0fdc3fmvp6p7))
- Provide feedback to your team on tasks they perform well and areas they could improve. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hsvfx57sqnre8b8dx04y9kc4))
