## Highlights

- The Demand Flexibility Service is a “peak shaving” product that pays people and businesses to use less energy when the electricity grid is at its most strained. It’s typically used on the coldest winter evenings; although it’s not used frequently, it pays well with a guaranteed price of £3/kWh for test events. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j0gjeqm1m5qefpak56n059h0))
- The UK electricity system is designed to ensure that there’s always enough power to meet demand. That demand changes constantly, from hour-to-hour (daily demand peaks in the evening) and month-to-month (yearly demand peaks in the winter). ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j0gjf191ksnqwdttebehv7yj))
- Last year’s peak demand of 58 GW was about [75% higher than average demand](https://www.nationalgrideso.com/document/283101/download) - that means we need infrastructure and generation capacity that may be used very rarely, if at all. This excess capacity (typically from fossil fuels) gets paid to sit mostly idle, which ultimately gets added to our bills. But it has historically been the only option to avoid blackouts on the coldest winter days. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01j0gjfe6bpkqr8p9kbdcac29s))
