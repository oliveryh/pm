## Highlights
- When we learn about something new, it's easy to fall into all sorts of rabbit holes that consume a lot of time and energy with a low return on investment. The world changes [faster and faster](https://www.dsebastien.net/developassions-newsletter-18-exponential-change/), and technology evolves all the time. If we're not careful, it's easy to spend time learning about things that will become obsolete *really* quickly. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01gtp259awp25320rreb2btgm4))
- When choosing whether to spend time learning something or not, it's useful to determine whether it is (mostly) stable knowledge or not. **Stable knowledge is knowledge that remains valid over a long period of time**. That is, at least years or even decades. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01gtp25tb6kxv33ywsfpdse2kj))
