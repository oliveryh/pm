## Highlights

- As Sam Bankman-Fried rose and fell, people outside of Silicon Valley began to hear about "effective altruism" (EA) for the first time. Then rifts emerged within OpenAI with the ouster and then reinstatement of CEO Sam Altman, and the newer phrase "effective accelerationism" (often abbreviated to "e/acc" on Twitter) began to enter the mainstream. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hkr8ja8zbysj2yrqsnkm51f1))
- Those who seek to place guardrails around technological development are no better than murderers, he argues, for putting themselves in the way of development that might produce lifesaving AI.
  This is the core belief of effective accelerationism: that the only ethical choice is to put the pedal to the metal on technological progress, pushing forward at all costs, because the hypothetical upside far outweighs the risks identified by those they brush aside as "doomers" or "decels" (decelerationists).
