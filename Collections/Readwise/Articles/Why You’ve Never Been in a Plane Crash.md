- Summary: The United States leads the world in airline safety, thanks in part to the way blame is assigned when accidents occur. The aviation industry has embraced the concept of a "blameless postmortem" as a standard practice in investigations. This approach aims to prevent future accidents by examining the overarching circumstances that led to the disaster, rather than focusing on assigning blame to individuals. By identifying systemic factors that increase the probability of mistakes, safety improvements can be made to ensure a high level of operational safety. This just culture recognizes that understanding why a mistake was made is more important than punishing individuals for the consequences.
