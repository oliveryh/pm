## Highlights

- But what is lost by following big B blogging? By chasing audience we lose the ability to be ourselves. By writing for everyone we write for no one. Too often I read things otherwise smart people have written for places like Fast Company and my eyes glaze over. Personal identity is necessarily watered down. Yes those places have large audiences but they’re shallow audiences. They don’t care about **you** at all. Your writing washes through their feeds like water. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hzcpd2y4bq54b5wppw513v02))
- Instead - I think most people would be better served by subscribing to small b blogging. What you want is something with YOUR personality. Writing and ideas that are **addressable** (i.e. you can find and link to them easily in the future) and **archived** (i.e. you have a list of things you’ve written all in one place rather than spread across publications and URLs) and **memorable** (i.e. has your own design, logo or style). Writing that can live and breathe in small networks. Scale be damned. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hzcpdpwg0s7escthrn28npas))
