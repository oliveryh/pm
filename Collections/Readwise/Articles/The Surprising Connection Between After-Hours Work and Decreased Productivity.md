- Summary: According to Slack's Workforce Index, employees who log off at the end of the workday are 20% more productive than those who feel obligated to work after hours. Taking breaks during the workday also improves productivity and well-being, yet half of desk workers rarely or never take breaks. The ideal amount of focus time is around four hours a day, and spending more than two hours a day in meetings can lead to feeling overburdened. Working after hours is associated with lower productivity and can indicate that an employee is overwhelmed with tasks. On the other hand, employees who choose to work outside of standard hours report no negative impacts and even a slight increase in wellness and productivity. It is important to create a culture of trust where employees feel comfortable asking for help prioritizing and have a balanced workday.

## Highlights

- Regardless of job tier, the research shows a “Goldilocks Zone” for the ideal balance of focus time, collaboration time, social connection, and downtime. On average, desk workers say the ideal amount of focus time is around four hours a day. More than two hours a day in meetings is the tipping point at which a majority of workers say they’re spending “too much time” in meetings, with a similar pattern emerging across all job levels. People who say they spend too much time in meetings are more than twice as likely to say they don’t have enough time to focus.
