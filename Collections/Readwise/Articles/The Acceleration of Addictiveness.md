- Summary: The document discusses the acceleration of addictiveness in society, highlighting how technological progress contributes to the creation of more addictive things. The author argues that the world is becoming more addictive, and unless social customs and antibodies can evolve at the same rate as technological progress, we will need to figure out for ourselves what to avoid and how to protect ourselves. The document also mentions the author's personal struggle with internet addiction and the need to say no to certain things in order to avoid addiction.

## Highlights

- We wouldn't want to stop it. It's the same process that cures diseases: technological progress. Technological progress means making things do more of what we want. When the thing we want is something we want to want, we consider technological progress good. If some new technique makes solar cells x% more efficient, that seems strictly better. When progress concentrates something we don't want to want — when it transforms opium into heroin — it seems bad. But it's the same process at work.
