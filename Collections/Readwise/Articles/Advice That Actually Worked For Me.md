## Highlights
- **Synthesize things as you read.** Just because you’ve read something, doesn’t mean you’ve understood it; your brain has to come up with its own encoding. Whatever understanding things is, it’s related to compression. Which implies that you want to read and then restate in your own words, so that your mind is forced to compress the thing. Ideally several times, in varying ways.
  Once you’ve done this, you are much more likely to retain the thing, and to actually grasp it; and if you’re struggling with this exercise, then you don’t understand the thing and should go back and look at it again. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kh4cxb73mqxfv4kg08g6v5nv))
- **Map out problems using logic trees.** This is a classic problem-solving and brainstorming technique, also known as [morphological analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morphological_analysis_(problem-solving)). It’ll be familiar to any consultant, as it’s 80% of their secret sauce. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01kh4d0x01ed4caw0444fagd1d))
