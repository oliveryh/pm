# How to Keep Engineers Out of Meeting Hell

![rw-book-cover](https://news.ycombinator.com/favicon.ico)

## Metadata

- Author: [[Brian]]
- Full Title: How to Keep Engineers Out of Meeting Hell
- Category: #articles
- Summary: Most meetings are not engaging for engineers because coding is more interesting and their impact in meetings is less tangible compared to coding. To make meetings more engaging for engineers, encourage them to ask the question, "How will we know when this meeting is over?" This helps determine if the meeting will be productive or a waste of time. Define clear outcomes for meetings to ensure they have a purpose and are relevant. Instead of forcing engineers into management-focused meetings, schedule decision meetings where they can bring their decisions and keep the agenda short. Choose meeting times that are less interruptive to engineers' focus and concentration. Limit meetings to only when necessary and consider asynchronous alternatives for communication. Conduct quick retrospectives to gather feedback and improve future meetings.

## Highlights

- A meeting outcome is not the same as an agenda. An agenda is an outline for how the meeting will be conducted, not what it will achieve. A great many pointless meetings have had wonderful well-structured agendas. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hqdvhhw4vnv5e0cezzc33atv))
- Meetings need a reason for existence, and that’s why they need a desired outcome. Make sure the outcome is clear, achievable, and relevant. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hqdvhm7d9d598yqwgf16awxv))
- To test if you have defined a useful outcome, ask yourself this question: **“How will the participants behave differently once the meeting is over?”** If you can’t think of anything, you don’t have a good outcome defined.
