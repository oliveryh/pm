# How to Run Probation

-   Their reflection: how do they feel the last 6 weeks have gone?
-   I share the feedback I've collected, and ask for their thoughts on it
-   A couple of stock questions:
	-   How good a fit do you feel Piclo is for somewhere you can see yourself long-term? In what ways is/isn't it a good fit?
	-   What would you change about your experience here, if you could change anything at all? No matter how realistic/unrealistic
- When you have in mind particular things they should improve on, wait to see if they come to these conclusions themselves first during the session before telling them what you had in mind
- Start to discuss goals:
	-   Why: clarity over what's expected to stay on the right trajectory
	-   Their thoughts: what do they want to be different in another 6 weeks time?
-   Suggest some based on the feedback I've shared
-   I don't normally finalise these in mid-probation; just start the convo then refine in our next 121 once they've had a bit more time to think about it
-   Anything else they'd like to ask or talk about
I like to send a message ~24hrs beforehand letting them know what to expect: high-level structure, and whether feedback is generally positive (if there are any moderate/big concerns then I try to succinctly forewarn them to avoid f2f surprises, e.g. "I have had some feedback about X, so I'd like to discuss how we can overcome this before the end of your probation")
