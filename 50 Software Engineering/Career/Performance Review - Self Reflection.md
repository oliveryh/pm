# Piclo - 2023/2024 Self Reflection

**Goal 1 - Personal Development - Evidence of line managees functional skills improving**

In this goal, my intention was to offer coaching in various formats to help develop the functional skills of my line managees. Since starting this goal, we regularly have "dev catch-ups" and this has been a good opportunity for me to hold small coaching sessions on topics such as Internationalisation and Testing principles.

While more recently the dev catch-ups have been focussed on sprint work, I've put a particular effort in attention to detail when scoping out tickets or leaving code reviews in an effort to lead by example. Since I have more time to draw a comparison, it looks like KM's abilities are improving, which may to a degree be as a result of that effort.

**Goal 2 - Role Based - Identify and implement a platform need that enables SO specific configuration**

In this goal, we wanted to improve System Operator's ability to configure the platform and running of competitions to their needs. Our team continues to iterate on ideas that enable SO configurability, such as create custom qualification rules and the ability to turn competition behaviours on or off.

The Competition Behaviours initiative was a good example of a SO configuration initiative that would have benefited from knowledge on various SO's needs, however I don't believe I was a champion in this respect. I mentioned organising a workshop, but the discovery for this initiative was largely led by Beatrice.

Out of the three goals, this was the hardest to see progress on, because our team's focus is largely revolving around SO configurability at the moment, and my contribution to that is a little more nuanced.

**Goal 3 - Personal Development - Improve the practice of change impact and release safety**

In this goal, I wanted to increase visibility of feature flags and the use of typing. While the use or extension of typing from myself or my team has been limited since starting this goal, I have tried to raise awareness of the complexities involved with the growing number of features in the codebase, and created a Feature Removal Assistant tool to improve the efficiency and safety with which we can improve that.

I believe this has demonstrated steps to actively improve the release process. In addition, our team is currently working on a "Files DevEx" initiative to reduce the manual, often error prone, process of releasing file templates.

It's a little too early to see the long-term impact on both these things, but I believe they show a concerted effort to improve how we manage releases.

**What went well**

It was nice to see that anyone can iterate on an idea and they'll have the platform to share it and gain feedback on it at various stages. With the feature removal assistant, I had the help of Stu to do a thorough review, the ongoing reviews from Allan to tweak, and the Dev & Tell / Show & Tell to spread the word of what I've created.

**What didn't go so well**

Piclo's positioning in the market and the organisational change that had to happen to transition had a big impact on my team's morale and work life balance. Being a pilot team to trial a production process change while also starting a new process of goals and goal reviews simultaneously has knocked down some of the ambitions I had for these goals.

On a more personal note, I think I became a little too outcome focussed, and didn't carve out enough time for personal development. We continue to try hard in my team to ensure that sprint's are set up so that there is always an opportunity to complete your sprint's tasks and have time to work on things outside the team (personal development or non-team ideas).

**Core Skills**

The new way of working helped accelerate a need for teams to keep things simple, and this has helped me develop my skill in simplicity. Through regularly framing scoping and discovery sessions in that way, I believe it's helped our team.

My direct interaction with our customers is still quite limited, although recently I did take part in a research session with ESO. Getting the first-hand experience of our features has helped me frame our conversations and I believe more participation in these sessions would help me achieve more ambitious goals relating to SO configuration.

**Function Skills**

I have several personal projects that allow me to get early experience on functional skills that I could apply to my role as a Full Stack Engineer. These projects have included the use of asynchronous python, and a Nuxt 3 project with Typescript.

It's through this interest in the python and javascript community that I found the library and means to complete the Feature Removal Assistant. I hope that continuing to engage in this area will help me bring other ideas that benefit the engineering team and grow my own function skills.

**Management Skills**

The weakest assessment in my management skills was Managing Conflict. While I haven't taken any formal training on this beyond LM training offered by Piclo, I do believe I've taken more opportunities to try to resolve conflict within my team and not leave so much of this to more senior piclonauts like JM and LW. During the recent way of working transition, I had a responsibility to reassure and build trust with KM and ER of the benefits. Having invested time with them during 1-to-1s to specifically address these conflicts, I feel like the practice has helped dramatically with this particular skill.

**Industry Knowledge**

My knowledge of the industry is still quite limited, despite the amount of time I have spent at Piclo. The role-based goal I had above relating to SO configuration was partly intended to help align that learning opportunity with a meaningful change, but it was also one of the goals that I've struggled the most to work towards. In terms of skills, I'd say it sits largely under various qualities around strategy.

**Code Documentation and Safety**

Secondly, I'd like to argue for and implement better use of typechecking in my team's features. It's a feature of python that has been introduced for some time, and I have seen the benefits of its use in personal projects. I'd quite like to use it professionally, especially if there's agreement that it benefits Piclo.

**Platform Performance**

A second technical goal that I have an interest in is performance and scaling. There is clearly a need within Piclo to address scaling issues in 2024.

While I generally feel confident that I'm well received as a line manager and lead engineer, I do have a concern that I'm not exercising my function skills muscle enough. Because I belong to one of the more junior product teams, I have fewer opportunities to collaborate and learn from the more senior engineers at Piclo.

I'd like my goals in 2024 to be positioned better so that I'm being scrutinised by those that I can learn and develop from. As much as helping grow my line managees is a worthwhile goal, I don't want to lose sight of the engineering aspect of my career.
