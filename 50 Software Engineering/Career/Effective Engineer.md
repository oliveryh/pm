# Social Etiquette

- **Get to the point**. Everyone's busy, make your point and get out their way
- **Don't explain why you're late**. Apologise and continue, no-one wants to know why you wasted their time
- **No Hello**. Don't start an instant message with hello, get straight to the point.
- **Challenge is Healthy** - Expressing our beliefs and opinions is important, and we should all have the room to do that. However, there's a fine line between sharing an opinion and sounding as though you're sharing an immovable fact. In a team, it's incredibly healthy for everyone to feel like they can challenge an opinion and potentially change it, or their own [^axioms]

[^axioms]: https://martinrue.com/my-engineering-axioms/

- Systematically push back on any meetings. Accept them only if you have a clear statement of who will drive it.

# Productivity

- **Sustainable Productivity** - The maximum rate of quality work output, without loss to the wellbeing of the developer
- **Leverage** - Leverage = Impact Produced / Time Invested. Optimizing for learning is high leverage.
- **Limit WIP** - The cognitive load required to keep track of loads of tasks can be tiring. Limiting WIP and ticking off tasks regularly is rewarding.
- **Riskiest Task First** - Start the riskiest task first, this will provide you time to get help
- **Double you time estimates** - Expect that every task will take at least 2 times what you anticipated, but try to get good at estimating how long tasks take.

# Prioritizing

- **Don't context switch** - The cost of context switching is high.

# Measure

Measuring is a great way to determine if you're making a positive impact, but can also convince others of the work you're doing.

- **Measure what you want to improve** - If you can't measure it, you can't improve it
- **Responsive Measures** - Updates quickly to give feedback on changes

# Iteration and Automation

Having a small feedback loop and iterating allows you to respond to change quicker.

- **Continuous Deployment** - Continuous deployment is high leverage. Removing the cognitive load of doing a manual process on a regular basis is very valuable.
- **Writing tools** - If you have to do something more than twice, write a tool to do it the third time
- **Shorten Loops** - Shorten your debugging and validation loops
- **Spend time on Deubgging Workflows** - Debugging is hard and time consuming. Upfront investments to shorten debugging loops are worth it
- **High Test Coverge** - Reduces build and site breakages and provides confidence when making changes
- **Don't Automate Decisions** - Automating mechanics is good. Automating decision making is bad.
- **Recover Quickly** - Leverage recovering quickly > leveraging preventing failures. (Ask for forgiveness not permission
- **Make Batch Process Idempotent** - Allow a batch process to be rerun without breaking a global state.

# Validation

- **Get feedback early** - As soon as possible, get feedback. This will allow you to reduce wasted time on assumptions.

# Operational Complexity

- **Keep No. Technologies Low** - Consider the skillset of your team and the upskill requirement. Control most technologies and perhaps try a new technology in an isolated part of the overall project.
- **Occam's Razor** - The simplest solution is often the right one.
- **Explore Second Solutions** - Trying to find a second solution to a problem often forces you to think differently and consider trade-offs.

# Learning

- **Compound Learning** - Work on complex tasks where you have a high learning rate.
- **Fast Growth** - You'll find fast growth in a company where No. Problems > No. Resources.
- **Don't be the smartest person in the room** - If you're the smartest person in the room then you're no longer learning.

# Team Growth

- **Invest in Onboarding**
- **Shared ownership of code** - Sharing ownership removes silos and encourages collaboration
