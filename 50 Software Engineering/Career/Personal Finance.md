# Background

## Funds

A pool of investor money benefitting from economies of scale, diversification, and flexibility. Can include: stocks & shares, bonds, cash equivalents, and property.

### Types of Funds

#### Open-Ended

Shares aren't fixed — new shares are created as investors add money and cancelled when they redeem.

#### Closed-Ended

Shares are fixed at the point the fund is established.

## Fees

- **Custody Fee** - Holding and safeguarding assets
- **Brokerage Fee** - Buying and selling assets
- **Trustee Fee** - Managing the fund's legal structure
- **Fund Management Fee** - Deciding which stocks to invest in
- **Ongoing Advice Fee** - If you engage a financial advisor

# Tips

## Get Rich Slowly

- Start early — a year's procrastination costs you at the end, not the beginning
- Invest regularly, but still enjoy your younger years
- Be patient — compound interest requires leaving money untouched

> Since I'm looking for an ethical portfolio, this may cost more due to the active work of screening fund components.

## Rules for Investing

1. Only invest what you're comfortable not accessing for 5–10 years
2. Keep a 3–6 month emergency fund
3. Pay off high-interest debt first
