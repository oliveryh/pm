## Performance Review Bias

https://www.cultureamp.com/blog/performance-review-bias

High level biases to avoid
- Recency bias - Putting emphasis on more recent contributions over those across the entire year
- Primacy bias - Putting emphasis on first impressions
- Halo / Horns effect - Allow a good or bad train to overshadow others
- Centrality bias - The tendency to rate in the middle or average of a scale to not be too extreme
- Leniency bias - When managing multiple people, it's tempting to be lenient to avoid hurt feelings if you manage peers
- Similar-to-me bias - Biasing those that are similar to me
- Idiosyncratic Rater bias - Rating things the assessor is good at lower as they have high standards or knowledge of how they may do a certain thing
- Confirmation bias - Giving more importance to feedback that corroborates their existing beliefs about that individuals performance
- Gender bias - Female presenting individuals tend to get feedback relating to their style or personality as opposed to skills
	- Specifically talking about situations, behaviors, and impacts rather than personality or style
- Law of small numbers bias - Incorrect belief that a small sample closely shares the properties of the underlying population
