# Motivation

Currently Oliver pays for most shared expenses and we operate from separate accounts. The goal is to move to a joint account as the primary account for shared spending, reducing friction and making finances fairer.

We have a Monzo joint account but Chloe prefers a more established bank — her concern is that Monzo is commonly used by scammers due to how easy it is to obtain a card, making it less trusted for serious joint finances.

# Ideas

## Joint Account Options

| Bank | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Nationwide | Already have a mortgage with them — could simplify relationship |
| Starling | Similar app experience to Monzo but stronger fraud controls and less associated with scams |
| Halifax / Lloyds | Traditional, widely trusted, good switching incentives |
| First Direct | Consistently rated highly for customer service |

Starling is probably the closest to Monzo in experience but with a better reputation for reliability.

## Requirements

- **Chloe:** strong fraud protection; not associated with scammers
- **Oliver:** good API access to export transactions for personal finance tracking
  - Alternative: use [Snoop](https://www.snoop.app/) via open banking API as a layer on top of any bank

## What to Run Through the Joint Account

- Mortgage / rent
- Utilities (gas, electric, water, internet)
- Groceries
- Eating out together
- Shared subscriptions (TV, music, etc.)
- Home maintenance and improvements
- Holidays and travel together
- Gifts for shared relationships (family, mutual friends)

## How to Fund It

- Agree a fixed monthly contribution from each person based on income split or 50/50
- Alternatively, both salaries go in and personal spending comes out via individual allowances — simpler but requires more trust and alignment
