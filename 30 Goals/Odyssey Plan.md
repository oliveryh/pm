# Questions

## Plan 1 - Current Trajectory in 5 Years

- I'd be working at Credit Suisse, and my career will be summarised mostly by hours worked and the knowledge that I've contributed to a company that could support many different things against my set of values
- I'd be living in SE London
- I'd never have committed to one physical discipline in particular and will have generally okay health but will still be suffering from various complaints
- I'll be earning well but won't have made many collaborative efforts with my colleagues, feeling generally isolated from my colleagues. This will remove the long term satisfaction from the job
- I'll be spending my weekends generally preparing for the working week
- I'll never really pick up a hobby, the biggest part of my interests will be focused around my day job
- I'll have accumulated many unfinished projects that have no real value in and of themselves
- I'll never make many more friends outside the TA Scheme / Uni Friends / GF
- My relationship will remain comfortable, but I won't have made the effort to ensure that I could truly share every aspect of my life with that person
- I won't have a spiritual relationship with myself
- I will not have any qualifications that make me hirable in the full range of disciplines that I have studied
- I'll still have a bad relationship with Reddit, YouTube , Porn etc.

## Plan 2 - Drastically Changed Trajectory in 5 Years

- I'll spend one or two more years at Credit Suisse to accumulate wealth and experience and then likely spend 3-12 months travelling and working on individual projects
- I will transition to an entirely vegan diet, including all the nutrients to have a healthy lifestyle
- I will have found a physical practice that allows me to set goals, be physically healthy, and possibly join a community to share experiences or exercise with (sports).
- I will move to a small-medium sized company that has employees that I can accept as more than acquaintances, people that share similar values. Not only this but I agree with the mission of the company.
- I will start engaging more with the communities around me, including my neighbours, local groups, or online.
- I will have a personal website, allowing me to have autonomy over my self promotion.
- I will have fostered a strong relationship, confident in the fact that big life decisions such as having children, etc will generally be known once entering my 30s.
- I'll be making regular donations of my time/money to charitable organisations
- I'll be travelling regularly, opting for slow travel or staycations where possible.
- I'll have adopted a lifestyle that is focussed on phone calls, one-on-one interactions, and real visceral experience as opposed to artificial replications of these most basic human needs.
