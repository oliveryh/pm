# Standard Operating Procedures

A Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is a documented, step-by-step process for completing a recurring task consistently and efficiently. The goal is to remove the need to make decisions about *how* to do something each time — freeing up mental energy for more important things.

## Why Use SOPs

- Consistency — same outcome every time, regardless of context or state
- Reduced cognitive load — no need to rebuild the process from scratch
- Easier delegation — clear enough for someone else to follow
- Continuous improvement — a written process is easier to iterate on

## What Makes a Good SOP

- A clear trigger (when does this process start?)
- Concrete, sequential steps
- Short enough to actually follow
- Stored somewhere accessible at the point of need
