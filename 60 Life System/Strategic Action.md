# GTD Workflow

## Capture

Capturing task should be done in either:

- Actionable (TickTick)
- Non-actionable (Linear)
- Time-bound (Google Calendar)

### Actionable (TickTick)

These are tasks that require action that is imminent (in the next week or so)

### Category

Each item can be categorised by the life categories mentioned previously.

### Non-actionable (Linear)

These are tasks that don't require imminent action, or collections such as suggestions for events, books, music etc.

### Time-bound (Google Calendar)

Things that don't require any task can simply be added to Google Calendar. If they have a task associated with them, these should be added to either Todoist/Trello.

## Engage

### Single-tasking

**Ziegarnik Effect**
People are more likely to remember incomplete tasks, rather than complete ones. Therefore this acts as motivation, to not leave tasks incomplete unnecessarily.

### Eating the Frog

The first tasks to schedule are incredibly important. They should consist of a quick win to ensure motivation starts well, but mostly made up of the most important and least appealing job.

### Time-boxing and Balancing Time

It is possible that some tasks I'm really engrossed in (work) can take over, and I end up sacrificing other aspects such as socialising and exercise. The problem is the lack of balance and lack of sustainability.

The proposed solutions to this are to:

- Time-Box Tasks: Stopping yourself abruptly after a particular time has passed and moving on
- Life Category limits: Work towards the expected hour limits for the day to ensure you leave time for other things

## Key to Life

## Requirements of a Strategic Action System

- Separate backlog from the current tasks (stay focused)
- Adjust to sudden important tasks
- Measure time commitement and actual time spent to create better acurates of how long something will take
- A feedback driven system where it's possible to have a retrospective
- The ability to capture all sorts of tasks in a single system

## Scheduling Upcoming Tasks

- Every 4 weeks (phase) transfer tasks from the backlog or create new tasks and place them into the upcoming phase
- Each week, consider the stage of that task you could aim to complete. For some tasks (*meditate everyday*), this will be the same for all four weeks of the phase
- Everyday, you can supplement your usual daily routines and ad-hoc tasks with the steps required to complete the week's tasks

**Tip**
 You should try to add no more than one task per life category. If you have completed **all** other tasks, then consider completing multiple tasks in one life category.
