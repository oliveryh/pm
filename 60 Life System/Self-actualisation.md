Self-Actualisation is the achieving of ones potential. In Maslow's Hierarchy of needs, it is the top of the pyramid, and as such, it is the hardest to achieve. The following discusses what is required to reach my potential.

# Task Dichomoty

Everything we strive for is split into two. The relaxing holiday abroad needs planning, the tasty homemade meal needing shopping for. Time spent doing particular things can generally be split into two categories:

1. **Inspired:** This the time you'll look back on, and what people often set goals to do. This time you want to **maximise**.
2. **Necessary:** These are the necessary actions of completing the inspired goals. This time you want to **minimise.**

## Inspired Time

- **Values:** These guide you and help make particular decisions but are not necessarily tangible. They help drive which goals to pursue and prioritise.
- **Goals:** These are long term and might take up to a few years to achieve.
- **Habits:** These are the day-to-day necessary actions to reach goals.

## Necessary Time

There are also actions that need to be completed to be an effective adult. These are usually uninspiring but necessary (cleaning the toilet, going food shopping). These can be split into the following:

- **Tasks:** One-off objectives that can be completed within up to an hour or two.
- **Routines:** Tasks that are recurring.

# Being in the right job

You don’t have to love your job. Jobs can be many things, but they’re also a way to make money. Many people live fine lives in okay jobs by using the money they make on things they care about.

This idea is also repeated in the Effective Altruism community for making change. They say that you should work in a high earning job so you can best supplement charitable organisations. Something that may be better serving than joining an NGO.

Perhaps this happens with time as well. Could a job in an industry I'm not in love with but provides me with a good income and time allow me to pursue something I'm passionate about.

# Convergent and divergent phases in life

Focus and confidence in what we do comes from experience. In order to gain this experience, we have to explore our options, try things out, and have the conviction to say "This is the best thing for me to spend my time on". Doing so stops us from being tentative and wondering what we're missing out on.

The Sephist [^the-sephist] explores this as a divergent phase of life where we're broading our inputs. We go into education, travel, or consider a career change. During a convergent phases we focus on a few of the things we may have discovered and invest in these.

> *"We grow fastest not by converging constantly onto some single point set from the start, but over cycles of divergence and convergence that come and go like seasons"* - **The Sephist**

It's important to remember that it's okay to experience both phases, and not believe you should be hustling all the time.

# Mental Airtime

The YouTuber “Better Ideas” talks about a concept of [Mental Air Time](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fvRDBpEliEU&list=WL&index=156&t=301s&pp=gAQBiAQB) whereby we struggle to concentrate in situations because we’re aware that we’re not the best version of ourself.

Since this thought is irrational, it requires a more emotional response as opposed to a rational narrative. The suggestion is to “submit” and trust that your future self has your best interests in hand.

# Resume vs Eulogy Virtues

Resume virtues bring you to the marketplace while eulogy virtues are the ones people speak about at your funeral.

> Character is defined by how deeply rooted you are. Have you developed deep connections that hold you up in times of challenge and push you toward the good? In the realm of the intellect, a person of character has achieved a settled philosophy about fundamental things. In the realm of emotion, she is embedded in a web of unconditional loves. In the realm of action, she is committed to tasks that can’t be completed in a single lifetime. Her capacity for empathetic understanding expanded. She lived in a state of steady, devoted love with Lewes, the kind of second love that comes after a person is older, scarred a bit and enmeshed in responsibilities. people on the road to inner light do not find their vocations by asking, what do I want from life? They ask, what is life asking of me? How can I match my intrinsic talent with one of the world’s deep needs? [^moral-bucket-list]
