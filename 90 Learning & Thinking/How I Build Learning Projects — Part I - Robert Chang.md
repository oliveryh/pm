## Choosing a Learning Project

### Prioritise Foundational Skills

- Become very good (top 25%) at **2+ things**
- **Prioritise foundational skills** as the rate of change of various frameworks / paradigms is too high
- Frameworks are not foundational skills, however the workflow patterns these tools enable are (Don't focus on Keras vs PyTorch, focus on shallow models vs deep models)

### Explore Adjacent Skills

![[1_WktvV-yPatZddbBBAGgbxg.webp]]

- Adjacent Discipline - coined by Steven Sinofsky
- Data Scientists who learn Data Engineering can take on bigger projects
- Focus on transferable skills

## Designing a Learning Plan

> Treat yourself not only as the student, but as the teacher

### Define Your Learning Objectives

- Follow unguided action will lead you to expend energy aimlessly
- Create clear learning objectives/goals before starting → breakdown in project milestones
- Potential learning of objectives
    - Adopting or making change in how I work
    - Achieve better understanding of problem
    - Generate learning material (for others)
- Develop a curriculum by gathering courses, articles etc. before beginning

> _"A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched up to make it work. You have to start over with a working simple system"_ - John Gall
