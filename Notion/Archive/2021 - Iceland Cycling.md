### Water
- It is recommended to drink approximately 2-3 Litres of water a day. [^british-cycling]
- We'll use some of this water to submerge the food pouches and then consume as a hot beverage
- Electrolytes can be used in order to help the absorbtion of H2O [^bikepacking-desert]
- Apparently it's possible to access streams in the interior? [^wikivoyage-iceland]
- You should avoid taking water from larger rivers, especially glacial rivers and glacial outwashes, which have a very high load of sediment, which will often not settle even if you leave it to stand. Small streams seem to be preferable for drinking [^masterly-inactivity]

### Flat Tire
**Remove the tire**
1. Quick release the tire and let out the remaining air out of the tire
2. Push the valve up into the tire
3. Use tire levers to pop out the wheel rim
4. Locate the puncture and remove the sharp object

**Using a Puncture Repair Kit**
5. Use the sandpaper to roughen up the area where the puncture is
6. Apply a thin layer of glue
7. Wait at least one minute for the glue to become sticky
8. Pump up the tire slightly to ensure the puncture repair was successful

**Replacing with a new inner tube**
9. Discard of the old inner tube and then simply replace with the new inner tube

!!! warning
We should be prepared to fix all conceivable defects to our bike during this time as a minor defect could be very costly
