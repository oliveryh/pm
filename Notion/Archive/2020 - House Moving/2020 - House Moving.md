# Candidate Houses

# House Requirements

- Large kitchen
- The ability to open and close windows fully
- Some outdoor space (small patio or garden)
- A communal space (e.g. couch or dining table)
- Space to keep a bike and equipment
- Food recycling facilities
- More than one toilet (multiple occupancy)
- Space to do basic calisthenics
- Area to work from home
- Gas hobs
- Dining room table

# Location

- Within 30-40 minutes walking from work
- Within 5 minutes walk to a well-linked tube station (that's on the night tube)
- Close to bike friendly tube lines
- Close to night tube
- Close to areas of interest (Shoreditch, Hackney)
- If using a tube to commute, [sub-surface lines](http://www.squarewheels.org.uk/bike/LUbikes) are better
- Walk home from work goes passed a reasonably good food shop
- In choosing between living with 0-1 people vs 2 or more people, remember that ascertaining responsibility will no longer be instantaneous with more than one roommate (“whose dishes are these?”).

# House Search Methodology

There are particular actions that help more than others while looking for accommodation. The following are what provided the most value for me:
- Creating an account on SpareRoom
    - Be explicit about requirements
    - Express some flexibility and demonstrate your interests
- Go into estate agents (whole rent)
    - Going to the estate agent in person will allow you to create better relationship of requirements
    - Allows you to negotiate discounts

Things that didn't really amount to anything:
- Attending the speed house-mating event
    - The selection was too varied for my requirements
- Using apps such as MoveBubble or Badi
- Looking on Facebook
