# Soft Questions
## Why Oxford?
[Why Oxford?](http://www.cs.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/msc-computer-science/why_oxford.html)
- Cross discipline research opportunities
    - Collaborations across disciplines are where some of most impact can be made and Oxford is setup to foster this cross-discipline collaboration.
        - Collegiate system
        - Tutorials
        - More intimate setting
- Given the recent skepticism in expert opinion, it is important to effectively communicate your findings
    - The tutorial system at Oxford provides an opportunity for one to build up an understanding of a topic while their view is challenged and developed on a regular basis
- Having the Oxford Internet Institute is a great resource to have given the increasing amount of influence the internet has on influencing our daily lives
    - Social networks
    - The scale and throughput of data that we're producing and how to manage this

## Why this Course?
- In my personal statement, I expressed an interest in sustainability
- For a large proportion of these issues you're dealing with a large number of inputs through the likes of sensors, or social data, and then drawing a conclusion from this
- I believe that topics such as concurrent algorithms and data structures and database systems implementation would provide a foundation in how to implement these things effectively
- With the amazing tools out there we can all download Tensorflow and run a neural net, but now the power lies in applying these techniques at scale on modern problem sets

## Why a Masters?
- My experience of my undergrad was very positive and at the time I wanted to go to industry. Industry had dropped me into the deep end in terms of technical ability and I'm heavily benefited from that
- I want to contribute to something that betters peoples lives. I currently work in finance and I believe I need the exposure and cross-disciplinary opportunities to find that niche that I can really contribute to and excel in. The Master's is not only a good chance to get that exposure, especially with the industry ties, but also to contribute to the academic world again.

## What are you looking to do after this course?
- Go into a research position either in industry or in academia that focuses on this niche
- I believe I have good skills in mathematical foundation, experimentation

## What do you think you would do a project on if given the opportunity?
- Take something at the core of society like transport or food production and work out how to optimise it
- Cost and energy effective design is at the core of sustainable projects

# Personal Statement Questions
- Core Mathematical Principles
    - Linear Algebra - Grounding to a large amount of statistics and statics that build up the understanding of many ML concepts
    - Combinatorics - The science of counting. I believe that quantifying interactions lead to this concept of modelling which is proven within CS to be a very valuable thing to do
- Computational complexity and optimisation
    - Complexity of Algorithms - Turing Machines, Complexity spaces
    - Advanced Topics in Algorithms - Bulk Synchronous Processor
        - Parallel Processors that you broadcast variables to, process in parallel, and then collect. Reminds me a lot about Spark
- Classifier Chains
    - The process in which explanatory variables are initially used to make a prediction
    - Those predictions are then used on additional classifiers
    - The structure of these chains aren't necessarily predefined at the start
- Why did IoT in particular grab your attention
    - The granularity and kind of data being captured
    - No longer relying on human input
    - All sorts of conclusions can be drawn from patterns in data and IoT is a good starting point to produce that large amount of data
- Tell me more about a kernel of an OS
    - Portion of the operating system code that is always resident in memory
    - Facilitates interactions between hardware and software components
- Which particular machine learning topics are you interested in
    - NLP
        - Being able to communicate with machines is at the core of programming langauges
        - Given the maturity of those languages we have now reached the point we can abstract away from that and communicate via NLP
    - Computer Vision - Another sense that can be explored using CS
- What in particular interests you about the concurrent algorithms, data structures, and database systems modules
    - In this past year I've been using spark a lot and have had first hand experience of making use of concurrent programming in pySpark and scala
    - I believe that embracing these kinds of technologies are how we will tackle the large quantities of data that are available to us
    - The algorithms we use, the design of the data structures and the types of databases we use all have profound implications on how performant our solutions will be and I'd like to explore this further
- Tell me more about the Oxford Internet Institute
    - Mutli-disciplinary
    - Social and computer science dedicated to the study of information, communication, and technology
