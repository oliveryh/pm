*P.A.R.A. stands for Projects — Areas — Resources — Archives*
> [!note] 📖
> **Definition: Project
**A series of tasks linked to a goal
*(e.g. app mockup, project plan, write blog post)*

> [!note] 📖
> **Definition: Area of Responsibility
**A sphere of activity with a standard to be maintained over time
*(e.g. Health, Finances, Travel, Hobbies)*

> [!note] 📖
> **Definition: Resource
**A topic of theme of ongoing interest
*(e.g. Coffee, Music, Gardening)*

> [!note] 📖
> **Definition: Archive
**Inactive items from the other three categories

# PARA Method Explained
## System Requirements
A Universal System for collecting requirements should be the following
- universal, encompassing any conceivable kind of information from any source 
- flexible, able to work with any project or activity you take on, now and in the future 
- simple, not requiring any time-consuming maintenance, cataloguing, tagging, or reorganizing beyond a bare minimum 
- actionable, integrating seamlessly with task management and project management methods 
- cross-platform, able to be used with any application, now existing or yet to be developed 
- outcome-oriented, structuring information in a way that supports the delivery of valuable work 
- modular, allowing different levels of detail to be hidden or revealed, depending on the needs of the current task 
- opportunistic, in the good sense, taking advantage of work already being performed, instead of requiring dedicated overhead time

## Timelines
### Project
- Well defined requirements and definition of success
- Has a deadline
- Allows you quantify the amount of workload you have
- The sense of completion is essential for motivation

### Area
- A standard to be maintained over time
- No end date or final outcome
- Requires a constant amount of attention
- Require mindfulness and balance, introspection and self-awareness

## Four is the Magic Number
- Research has shown that there is a natural limit to cognitive processes, from working memory to object-tracking, to rapid enumeration
- We should limit our hierarchies of information to 4 layers deep

# Goals, Projects and Tasks
At a high level, we should be focusing on Goals. Projects and tasks that are associated with a particular goal should be given priority. There are of course tasks that won't be attributed to a particular goal, and these are often important. (paying rent, food shopping etc.). These will largely be captured in Todoist
- Goals - Completed in a few weeks solid work within a quarter
- Projects - Completed in a few days work within a two week period
- Tasks - Completed in a few hours work within a day
