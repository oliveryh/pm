# Setting Up a Productivity System
My steps for a productivity system can be summarised as the following:
1. Determine a list of life categories and their priorities
2. Create attainable SMART goals that fit within those categories
3. Make a list of habits you'll need to do to achieve those goals
4. Complete these tasks
5. Track and review progress

## 1. Life Categories
Life categories could be thought of as your roles in life. You being an employee, a son, a human being, and of course yourself. I say 'yourself' because not all of these revolve around a relationship with other people, but rather an espect of your own wellbeing as a 'life category'.
As an employee, this could be thought of as **Finances**. You're role is professional, but ultimately this is contributing to your finances and the money you make. As a son, a friend or a partner, you could think of this as **Relationships**. My list of 8 life categories are the following:
- Physical - Development and expression of ones body (exercise, sleep, diet, sex)
- Mental - Understanding of the mind (meditation, self-understanding, self-care)
- Relationships - Development of interactions with others (friends, family, romantic relationships)
- Growth - Technical development of the mind (reading, learning, skill acquisition)
- Lifestyle - Development and expression of surroundings and assets (grooming, chores, possessions)
- Career - Development of my ability to contribute to a companies success.
- Finances - Accumulation of investments and wealth (career, investments)
- Avocation - Expression of myself (hobbies, creativity)
- Contribution - Betterment of my environment and the world's population (volunteering, donations, assistance)
- Travel - Better understand other people, cultures, and landscapes.

You'll notice that the ordering isn't alphabetical, but rather the order of importance. By doing this, you're able to have a fallback if you have two things that require your time. For example, if you can only do one task and it's the choice between getting more sleep or reading. **Physical** comes before **Growth**, therefore we'd pick the sleep.
Similarly, you can have implicit importance of items within a life category. **Relationships** may have the implicit category of 'family' before 'friends' and 'friends' before 'colleagues'. Therefore, once again, if you can only do **one** activity, and you want it to be a **Relationship** one, you can appropriately decide with this ordering.
## 2. Creating SMART Goals
Now that we have our life categories. We should pick goals that we can assign to this life category. For example, you may finish a book to contribute to **Growth** or try achieve a particular weight to contribute to **Physical**.
While there may be some overarching goal like 'be a calisthenics god', I realise that I cannot achieve this in a small enough time to effectively with towards this.
It's important to note, that you should only have one or two main goals for each category. I've learnt that single tasking is very important, it allows you to get immersed in something you're doing.
## 3. Make a List of Tasks to Achieve your Goals
Let's use calisthenics as and example. Suppose I have the following goal.
> Be able the squat 40kg for 3x5 rounds

I would add the 2-3 pomodoros required to carry out a workout routine each day to achieve that. Additionally, I'd keep track of the measurable metric of the goal.
Or alternatively, I want to finish reading a book. I'd possibly add 2 pomodoros of time to read the book each morning before work.
## 4. Track and Review
At the end of each day, we can recognise the tasks that contributed to a goal. Perhaps just as importantly, we can see the number of tasks we achieved that day.
