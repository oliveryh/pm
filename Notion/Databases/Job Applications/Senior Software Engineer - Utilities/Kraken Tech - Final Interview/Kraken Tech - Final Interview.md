## Project Management

### Ideal Project Management Contribution

### **Succinct Customer-First Engineering Bullet Points**

- Understand the customer problem before writing code.
- Validate assumptions through customer feedback and data.
- Design solutions around customer workflows, not internal structures.
- Prototype early; iterate with real users.
- Optimise for clarity, simplicity, and reduced friction.
- Build flexible, scalable components that support evolving needs.
- Collaborate closely with customer-facing teams.
- Measure success with customer-centric metrics.
- Continuously refine based on post-launch usage and feedback.


1. Discovery and Scoping
    1. Ask clarifying questions
    2. Validate assumptions through customer feedback and data
    3. Identify who the user is
    4. Identify what problem we’re solving
    5. Pushback if scope is unclear
2. Break down work
    6. What dependencies are there, are do they sit with other teams

# Jordan’s Notes

- “People” are the secret source behind Kraken’s success
- The organisation is “left with the problem of humans” when technology problems are solved
- Kraken’s systems handle many back-office tasks, enabling leaders to shift their focus from processes and control to getting the best out of humans
- Kraken’s success
    - Agility
    - Pace over Perfection
    - Automatic Trust
        - Servent leadership
    - Psychological safety
    - Workforce is empowered to be decision-makers, leaders, focusing on coaching and mentoring rather than control
- Three key things
    - Trust is automatic vs trust needs to be earned
    - Physchological safety vs Culture of fear
    - Empowerment vs Control
- Four Cultural Values
    - Freedom and Responsibility
        - Empowerment, Kindness, Psychological Safety, Servant Leadership
    - Get Shit Done
        - Ownership, Accountability, Problem-Solving, Wellness
    - Customer Obsession
    - Purple
        - Fun, Joy, Anti-Corporate, Building Community, “Kraken Magic”
