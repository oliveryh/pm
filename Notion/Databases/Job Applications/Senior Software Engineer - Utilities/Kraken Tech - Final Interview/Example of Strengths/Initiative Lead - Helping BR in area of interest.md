# Context
- We often had designers and PMs take a sprint or two in advance of development work to look at customer research etc
- When the team started working on a topic, we found that some engineering input could have been helpful earlier on
- Knowledge was becoming siloed as people became “Go-to”s

# Action
- I trialed a “Initiative Lead” concept where engineers would rotate being in earlier discussions with Designers/PMs
- This involved talking to engineers within my team and understanding what responsibilities might be helpful to bring into the role

# Result
- **Empowerment, Ownership - **Engineers felt empowered to steer the direction of our work, and felt more ownership of it
- **Wellness** - Spread out engineer demand
- **Growth and Development - **I was line managing an individual who expressed an interest in GIS/Geographic/Maps, and leading an initiative on this topic when it came up gave them an opportunity to push their leadership capabilities

# Learnings
- Gathering input from people about how to shape a role was very valuable
- Trialing a process and assessing if/how it’s working is a good


# 🚩Red Flags
- Creating a process that didn’t need to exist
